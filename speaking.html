1.1 Work

1.1.1 What work do you do?

  • work for Intel as a software engineer

1.1.2 Why did you choose to do that kind of job?

  • majored in this, promising industry and popular, still is today
  • challenging / get sense of achievement all the time

1.1.3 What do you do in that job?

develop software or applications by writing source code test and debug if not working as expected may sounds interesting to some people sometimes can be really difficult and time-consuming

1.1.4 What is you typical working day like?

start with riding my moped to the company have breakfast at the dining hall when I get to work, the first thing is checking email and update my progress start coding or discuss with other team members about designs other routines get off work around six p.m. if no need to work overtime

1.1.5 Do you use any equipment in you work?

computers of course some other hardware needed for the project

1.1.6 Do you like your job?

passionate about my job on so many different levels super challenging make me feel quite fulfilled and accomplished fast developing industry, new technologies come out everyday, learning new things good salary and benefits and paid vacation promising career path because my boss think highly of my work

1.1.7 Would you recommend that job to other people?

only to those who like challenges able to work under pressure with schedules and deadlines must be eager to learn

1.1.8 What work do young people in your country prefer to do?

met quite a few young people thinking about starting their own businesses, open a shop or a small company related to their major confident and independent may not succeed, courage deserves praise

1.1.9 Do you think that you are overloaded with work?

yes in the beginning years of my career now I'm experienced, find it not that difficult or I just get used to it

1.1.10 What's the most difficult part of it?

in first several years, feel stressed, pushed by schedule and deadline now it's more of interpersonal relationships need to communicate and cooperate with others when dealing with more complex systems

1.1.11 Do you plan to continue doing this type of work?

striving to become a software architect do more design work in the future

2 PART II & III

2.1 Describe a course you learned at middle school, university or evening class.

You should say: what the course was what you did at school, university or evening class who taught the course and explain what you learned from the course.

2.2 Describe an intelligent person you know.

You should say: who this person is when and where you first met him or her what kind of person he or she is and explain why you think this person is intelligent.

2.2.1 Do yo think it's important for people to be intelligent?

2.2.2 Is it the only thing that is important?

2.2.3 Do you think nowadays people need to be more intelligent than people in the past?

2.2.4 Do you think computer have "intelligence"?

2.2.5 What do you think is the difference between the intelligence of a computer and that of a human?

2.2.6 Do you think computers might one day be more intelligent than humans?

2.2.7 Do you think there are more highly intelligent children nowadays, compared to the past? (Why?/Why not?)

2.2.8 Do you think it's best for these highly intelligent children to go to normal schools or do you think they should go to special schools?

2.2.9 Do you think very intelligent people and not so intelligent people are born that way and their intelligence cannot be changed during their childhood?

2.2.10 Do you think intelligent people are happy?

2.2.11 Do you think play games can help children become more intelligent? (Why?/Why not?)

2.2.12 What do you do for play when you were a child?

2.3 Describe a restaurant you like/that impresses you.

You should say: where this restaurant is what type of food the restaurant has why you go to this restaurant and explain why you like this restaurant/this restaurant impresses you.

2.3.1 What are some reasons why people eat out?

2.3.2 Does it give people more status to eat in a restaurant rather than eat at home?

2.3.3 Do people now go to restaurants more than before? (Why?/Why not?)

2.3.4 Do you prefer to eat in a restaurant or at home?

2.3.5 What's the difference between eating at home and eating in a restaurant?

2.3.6 Is there any difference between home-cooked food and food in restaurants?

2.3.7 Which food do you think is healthier, restaurant food or home-cooked food?

2.3.8 How would you introduce a foreigner to the food and the food culture in your country?

2.3.9 In your opinion, what is a "healthy diet"?

2.3.10 What are some examples of unhealthy food?

2.3.11 Do you think children should learn to cook?

2.3.12 How should a person teach a child to cook?

2.3.13 When buying food (such as in a supermarket or food market), what do you think people pay attention to?

2.4 Describe a situation (or a time) when you helped someone.

You should say: what the situation was who he person was how you helped them and explain how you felt after helping them.

2.4.1 Do you like helping others?

2.4.2 In your view, should children be taught to help others?

2.4.3 How can we encourage children to help others?

2.4.4 Do you think people are less willing to help others these days, compared to the past? (Why?/Why not?)

2.4.5 Do people today trust others as much as they used to in the past?

2.4.6 How do people in your community help one another?

2.4.7 How do students, such as high school students, help each other?

2.4.8 How can charitable organizations help people?

2.4.9 What are some examples of such organizations in your country?

2.4.10 What do you think are the benefits of having unpaid volunteer workers?

2.4.11 How do you think the volunteers themselves benefit?

2.4.12 Should professionals be hired rather than using volunteer workers?

2.4.13 Do you think international aid, (e.t., from one national government to another), is important?

2.4.14 Can you give any examples of international aid?

2.4.15 Do you agree that all countries should come together to help solve some of the big problems that they have?

2.5 Describe an unforgettable advertisement (that you saw or heard or liked).

You should say: where you saw or heard it what kind of advertisement it was what the contents of the advertisement was (or, what product or service was advertised) and explain how you felt when you saw or heard this advertisement / why you like it.

2.5.1 In general, what are the pros and cons of advertising?

2.5.2 Do you think there's too much advertising in our daily lives?

2.5.3 Do people in your country like advertisements?

2.5.4 In what ways do advertisements influence people?

2.5.5 What are the different forms of advertising that we have in society today?

2.5.6 Which of those do you think is the most effective means of advertising?

2.5.7 Do you think most advertisements achieve their purpose? (Why?/Why not?)

2.5.8 What types of advertisements do people remember most?

2.5.9 Some people say that the high cost of advertising adds to the price of products and that if advertising was banned, this would make products cheaper. Do you agree?

2.5.10 Do you think the government should have more (or, stricter) control over (or, regulations concerning) advertising?

2.5.11 Do children pay attention to (or, like) advertisement?

2.5.12 What do you think is the impact of advertising on children?

2.5.13 Do you think advertising a certain product can ever have negative effects?

2.5.14 What about adverting tobacco and alcohol - do you think these products should be advertised at all (or, regulated)?

2.5.15 Do you think the brand of a product is important to people?

2.5.16 For the producer, what are the advantages of having a well-known brand name?
